🔥 POST-FIRE SAFETY PROTOCOLS 🔥

🏢 FIRE ASSEMBLY POINTS

After a fire, always proceed to designated assembly points. These fire-safe zones provide adequate distance from damaged buildings and allow emergency responders to account for all evacuees.

📏 SAFE DISTANCE FROM FIRE DAMAGE

Maintain a safe distance of at least 100 feet from fire-damaged buildings. Hot surfaces, toxic gases, and structural instability make close proximity extremely dangerous.

⚠️ CRITICAL FIRE SAFETY REMINDERS ⚠️

🚫 NEVER RE-ENTER FIRE-DAMAGED BUILDINGS:

Structural integrity is compromised. Carbon monoxide, smoke, and hidden flames may still pose deadly threats. Wait for professional fire department clearance.

👁️ WATCH FOR HAZARDOUS MATERIALS:

Fire can release toxic chemicals, asbestos, and other hazardous materials. Avoid contact with water run-off from fire scenes.

🎯 FOLLOW EMERGENCY CREW INSTRUCTIONS:

Fire officials will direct you to appropriate areas. Never return to buildings until officially cleared by fire department or building inspectors.

📱 ACCOUNT FOR EVERYONE:

Do a headcount at assembly points. Report any missing persons to emergency responders immediately.
